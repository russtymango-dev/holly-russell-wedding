Sonja Ballard (From Holly)

Dear Mum,

I’ve tried about a hundred times to write this without crying, and I don’t think it’s possible — because when I think about you and what you’ve done for me, it hits me in that deep, grateful place that words never fully reach. But I want to try, because you deserve to hear it.

You’ve been supportive in every sense of the word — not just in the obvious ways, but in the quiet, constant ways that hold a person together across their whole life. You’ve been the person who notices what I need before I say it. The person who gives without keeping score. The person who stands behind me like a safety net, but never makes me feel small. You’ve always found that balance — loving me fiercely, but still letting me become myself.

When I look back over my life, you’re in every part of it. Not as a background figure — as a steady presence. A voice. A warmth. A home. You’ve been kind when I needed softness, strong when I needed someone to take the wheel for a moment, and honest when I needed grounding. And no matter what stage of life I’ve been in — child, teenager, adult — you’ve always made me feel like I belong with you.

I know I’m not perfect. I know I’ve been dramatic, stubborn, emotional, stressed, messy — all the things people are when they’re trying to figure themselves out. And you’ve loved me through all of it. You’ve never made me feel like love is something I have to earn by being easy. You’ve made love feel safe.

Today, as I get married, I’m carrying so much of you with me. The way you love. The way you show up. The way you build a family with consistency and care. You have shaped what I believe a home should feel like. You’ve shaped the kind of wife I want to be, the kind of person I want to keep growing into, and the kind of life I want to build.

And Mum… thank you for being the person who makes things happen. The organiser, the helper, the calm in the chaos. Thank you for every phone call, every check-in, every “do you need anything?” Thank you for loving Russell too, and for welcoming him with an open heart. It means more than you know, because loving me also meant making space for the love of my life, and you’ve done that beautifully.

I love you. I admire you. And I hope today feels like a celebration of you too — because the truth is, I wouldn’t be who I am without you.

All my love,
Holly

Kevin Ballard (From Holly)

Dear Dad,

I don’t think you realise how often I’ve thought, “I’m lucky I got him as my dad.” Not in a casual way — in a real, life-shaping way. Because you’re the kind of father people hope for. The kind that shows up. The kind that would do anything. The kind that makes you feel safe just by being there.

You’ve always been steady for me. Even when life has been busy, even when things have been hard, even when I’ve been stressed or emotional or all over the place — you’ve never made me feel like I’m too much. You’ve been patient. You’ve been supportive. You’ve been the person I can rely on, and I can’t tell you how much that’s meant in the seasons where I’ve needed it most.

Some dads say “I love you” with words. You’ve said it with actions my whole life. With help. With effort. With showing up. With being willing to do the hard, boring, practical stuff that makes someone’s life easier. And with the way you’ve always treated me like I matter.

Dad, I want you to know that I notice who you are. Not just as “my dad,” but as a human being. You’re genuinely good. You care about people. You’d go out of your way for someone without needing credit. You’ve got a heart that’s bigger than you let on sometimes, and you’ve built a family that feels held by it.

And now, on my wedding day, I’m thinking about all the moments that brought me here — and you’re in so many of them. You helped shape what I believe love is meant to look like: consistent, loyal, protective, and real. You helped shape what I believe a man should be: kind, dependable, and willing to show up for the people he loves.

I love you so much. I’m proud to be your daughter. And I hope today feels like a celebration for you too — because you’ve played such a huge part in the life I’m building.

Love always,
Holly
